Global Obesity and Calorie Supply Visualisation

Exploring how global obesity trends relate to food consumption and energy intake from 1990 to 2022

Global Overview

Obesity has become one of the most pressing global health challenges of the 21st century. Since 1980, worldwide obesity rates have more than doubled, affecting both developed and developing nations. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), over 650 million adults were classified as obese in 2022 which represents nearly 16% of the global population. The rise of obesity is strongly linked to changes in diet, lifestyles, and urbanisation. While obesity was once considered a problem only in high-income countries, it is now increasingly prevalent in low and middle income nations as well.

Calorie Intake and Obesity Relationship (2022)

Insights

There is a clear positive relationship between calorie supply and obesity rates. High-income regions such as Europe and the Americas cluster toward higher values, while low-income regions remain on the lower end of both calorie intake and obesity.

This visualisation highlights how dietary energy availability is a major factor behind regional differences in obesity prevalence. The regression line shows a strong upward correlation between calorie consumption and obesity rates.

Gender Distribution of Obesity (2022)

In 2022, females had a higher average obesity rate globally (around 19.2%) compared to 13.5% among males. This reflects both biological and lifestyle differences that influence weight gain patterns worldwide.
